## v1.0

- **Date:** 2026-06-08
- **Amendments:** AOC-AMD-0001
- **Summary:** Establishes constitutional continuity after constitutional enforcement by making law changes, authority changes, ownership changes, constitutional exceptions, and transitional domains governed by amendment procedure.
- **Breaking Changes:** None
- **Migration Notes:** Existing laws and transitional authorities remain unchanged. Future constitutional changes must be recorded in the amendment catalog and version history.


## v2.0

- **Date:** 2026-06-08
- **Amendments:** AOC-AMD-0002
- **Summary:** Establishes Constitutional, Governance, Runtime, and Operational capability classes; catalogs capability definitions and founding assignments; governs lifecycle, delegation, restrictions, suspension, revocation, retirement, and evolution; and adds fail-closed CI and release scanners.
- **Breaking Changes:** Authority existence no longer implies power. Capability power is constitutionally explicit, assigned, and deny-by-default.
- **Migration Notes:** Repository governance changes only. No runtime authorization or policy engine is introduced. Future capability changes require Type B or Type C amendments.


## v3.0

- **Date:** 2026-06-08
- **Amendments:** AOC-AMD-0003
- **Summary:** Establishes Constitutional, Governance, Runtime, and Operational policy classes; catalogs policy authorities; governs lifecycle, inheritance, delegation, exceptions, conflict resolution, and evolution; and adds fail-closed CI and release scanners.
- **Breaking Changes:** Capability possession no longer implies unconstrained exercise. Every canonical capability must have active applicable policy coverage.
- **Migration Notes:** Repository governance changes only. No runtime policy or decision engine is introduced. Future policy changes require Type B or Type C amendments.
