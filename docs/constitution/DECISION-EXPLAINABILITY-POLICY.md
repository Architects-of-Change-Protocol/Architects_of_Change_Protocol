# Decision Explainability Policy

**Constitution Version:** v10.0

## Explanation rule

Every approved decision must have a complete, human-auditable explanation. An explanation describes the constitutional basis of the outcome; it does not expose secrets, fabricate reasoning, or substitute narrative for evidence.

Every explanation must provide the Decision ID, Evidence Used, Policies Applied, Capabilities Used, Authority Chain, Outcome, Reasoning Summary, governing amendment, and completeness status.

## Disclosure and integrity

Protected evidence may be referenced by digest or stable identifier, but omission of sensitive content must not conceal the policy, capability, authority chain, outcome, or existence of material evidence. Explanations are append-only; corrections are versioned and retain prior text for audit.
