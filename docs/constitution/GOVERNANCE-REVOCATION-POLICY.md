# Governance Revocation Policy

**Constitution Version:** v13.0

## Purpose

A governance revocation permanently removes the current governance effect of a mandate or governance act. Revocation preserves the historical record.

## Valid revocation causes

- `Fraud`
- `Invalid Consensus`
- `Invalid Standing`
- `Invalid Mandate`
- `Scope Violation`
- `Conflict Of Interest`
- `Evidence Failure`
- `Constitutional Override`
- `Governance Decision`

## Constitutional rule

Revocation prevents future reliance on a governance act but preserves the historical record. A revoked governance act may not be reactivated. Evidence and decision reference are required for all revocations.
