# Voting Constitution

**Constitution Version:** v15.0

## Constitutional purpose

**Voting** is a constitutionally governed process through which eligible actors formally express preference regarding a governance motion, proposal, mandate, decision, or constitutional artifact. Voting answers **how eligible actors formally express constitutional will**. Voting is not consensus, governance, decision, or execution.

## Sovereign voting rule

A governance motion may exist, but only a voting authority cataloged in `VOTING-AUTHORITIES.md`, operating under its eligibility policy, weight policy, and delegation policy, may constitute a legitimate voting act.

The constitutional chain is:

`Authority → Capability → Policy → Standing → Claim → Trust → Verification → Reputation → Attestation → Consensus → Governance → Voting → Decision → Action`

Voting may express constitutional will only as a traceable governed output. It never replaces required standing, evidence, policy coverage, trust evaluation, verification requirements, reputation signals, attestation endorsements, consensus agreement, or governance authority, and it never directly authorizes action.

## Voting legitimacy

A voting act is legitimate only when all of the following are traceable:

1. a canonical voting authority and owner;
2. a valid motion satisfying the motion policy;
3. eligibility validation under the governing eligibility policy;
4. weight assignment under the governing weight policy;
5. delegation validation where delegation is exercised;
6. every lifecycle transition, expiration, challenge, and revocation; and
7. the current voting status separately from preserved historical records.

No vote may be cast without eligibility validation. No vote may carry weight without weight policy validation. Delegation transfers voting expression only; it does not transfer authority ownership.

## Constitutional properties

Voting is motion-bound, eligibility-gated, weight-governed, delegation-bounded, lifecycle-controlled, expirable where cataloged, challengeable where cataloged, revocable where cataloged, historically preserved, and constitutionally evolvable. A vote never transfers authority ownership. A vote never copies expression from one subject, motion, or authority to another.

## Voting ≠ Consensus

Voting and consensus are constitutionally distinct:

| Votes Cast | Eligibility Valid | Weight Valid | Consensus Established | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| No | No | No | No | No expression; no agreement |
| Yes | No | No | No | Votes cast without eligibility; constitutionally invalid |
| Yes | Yes | Yes | No | Valid votes cast; consensus threshold not met |
| Yes | Yes | Yes | Yes | Valid votes contribute to established consensus |

All states are constitutionally valid. Decision authorities may consume voting and consensus acts as independent inputs.

## Voting ≠ Governance

Voting may be one governance mechanism. Governance is broader than voting. Voting expresses constitutional preference. Governance converts legitimate collective will into collective authority. Voting is an instrument of governance, not governance itself.

## Vote Weight ≠ Authority

Weighted votes may influence governance outcomes. Weight reflects constitutional eligibility criteria. Weight never creates constitutional authority. No weight assignment grants authority.

## Voting is an input, not a bypass

Voting may influence governance decisions. Voting may not override:

- Authority
- Capability
- Policy
- Standing
- Claim Evidence
- Verification Requirements
- Reputation Signals
- Attestation Endorsements
- Consensus Requirements
- Governance Authority

Voting is a governed input to governance. It is never a constitutional bypass.

## Scope boundary

This framework governs voting legitimacy and constitutional records only. It does not implement a voting engine, election runtime, federation runtime, blockchain voting, token voting, treasury voting, or execution runtime.
