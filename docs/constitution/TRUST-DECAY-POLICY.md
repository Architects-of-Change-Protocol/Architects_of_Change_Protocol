# Trust Decay Policy

**Constitution Version:** v13.0

## Decay rule

Trust may decay because of time, inactivity, evidence expiration, superseded claims, or governance events. Decay is a governed recalculation of **current confidence** only. It never deletes, rewrites, or conceals prior confidence, evidence, evaluations, or decisions.

A decay event records its trigger, prior confidence, calculation policy, resulting confidence, evaluator, time, evidence and decision references. Decay cannot increase confidence; an increase requires a new evidence evaluation. Crossing a policy floor requires suspension or reevaluation rather than silent deletion.
