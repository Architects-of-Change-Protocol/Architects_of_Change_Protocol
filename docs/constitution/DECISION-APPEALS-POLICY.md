# Decision Appeals Policy

**Constitution Version:** v1.0

## Appeal right

Only a decision whose catalog entry says `Appealable: Yes` may be challenged through this policy. Non-appealable constitutional decisions remain reviewable only by a superseding Type C amendment. Filing an appeal does not itself approve, reject, revoke, or execute a decision.

## Required appeal record

Every appeal must contain:

- a unique `APL-NNNN` Appeal ID;
- a known Decision ID;
- specific, non-empty Grounds;
- traceable Evidence;
- a Resolution;
- a valid lifecycle Status; and
- the amendment or constitutional authority governing the resolution.

Valid grounds include evidence error, evidence omission, policy misapplication, capability or authority defect, procedural defect, fraud, and constitutional conflict. Resolution must be `Approved`, `Rejected`, or `Revoked` and must produce the matching lifecycle transition.

## Appeal registry

|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

No decision appeals are recorded at v5.0.

## Independence and traceability

An appeal reviewer must not invent new authority. The resolution must cite the challenged decision, evidence considered, policies applied, reviewer capability, authority chain, and resulting explanation update.
