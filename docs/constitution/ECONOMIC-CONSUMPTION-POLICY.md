# Economic Consumption Policy

**Constitution Version:** v16.0

## GCU and SCU definitions

**GCU (Governance Compute Unit)** is the constitutional unit of measurement for governance activity. GCU measures the constitutional cost of governance-level computation, proposals, motions, decisions, and constitutional artifacts. GCU is a governance accounting primitive, not a currency.

**SCU (Sovereign Compute Unit)** is the constitutional unit of measurement for sovereign system activity. SCU measures the constitutional cost of runtime activity, protocol operations, and sovereign execution. SCU is a sovereignty accounting primitive, not a currency.

GCU and SCU are constitutional economic primitives denominated by their respective authorities (ECO-0009 and ECO-0010). They do not represent market value, staking positions, or financial instruments.
