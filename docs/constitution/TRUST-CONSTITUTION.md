# Trust Constitution

**Constitution Version:** v16.0

## Constitutional purpose

**Trust** is a governed confidence signal derived from evidence, behavior, history, and constitutional evaluation. Trust answers **how much confidence should be assigned** to an eligible claim or governed participant. Trust is not authority, capability, policy, standing, claim, decision, reputation, or permission to act.

## Sovereign confidence rule

Claims may exist, but only a trust type cataloged in `TRUST-AUTHORITIES.md`, evaluated under its evidence policy, issued by its authorized authority, and maintained through the constitutional lifecycle may influence governed confidence.

The constitutional chain is:

`Authority → Capability → Policy → Standing → Claim → Trust → Decision → Action`

Trust may influence a decision only as a traceable confidence input. It never replaces required standing, evidence, policy coverage, decision authority, or explainability, and it never directly authorizes action.

## Trust legitimacy

A trust signal is legitimate only when all of the following are traceable:

1. a canonical trust authority and owner;
2. valid standing and a valid claim for the subject evaluated;
3. evidence satisfying source, weight, integrity, recency, minimum, and calculation rules;
4. an authorized issuer and immutable trust record;
5. a valid lifecycle path through `Pending Evaluation` before `Active`;
6. every decay, suspension, reactivation, revocation, retirement, and decision reference; and
7. the current confidence value separately from preserved historical values.

No trust signal may become `Active` without evidence evaluation. No trust may be self-issued unless its issuance rule explicitly authorizes self-issuance.

## Constitutional properties

Trust is evidence-backed, purpose-bound, non-transferable by default, time-aware, decaying where cataloged, suspendable, revocable where cataloged, historically preserved, and constitutionally evolvable. Transfer means a new authorized evaluation and record; it never means copying confidence from one subject, context, claim, or authority to another.

## Scope boundary

This framework governs trust legitimacy and constitutional records only. It does not implement a trust, reputation, voting, federation, identity, verification, governance, policy, decision, claims, authentication, or authorization runtime.
