# Economics Constitution

**Constitution Version:** v17.0

## Constitutional purpose

**Economics** governs how value is defined, allocated, consumed, transferred, settled, and audited within and across sovereign systems. Economics answers **how sovereign constitutional systems govern value without collapsing value into currency**. Economics is not accounting, treasury, payments, runtime billing, or tokenization.

## Sovereign economics rule

An economics act may exist, but only an economics authority cataloged in `ECONOMICS-AUTHORITIES.md`, operating under its rights policy, obligations policy, asset policy, consumption policy, settlement policy, treasury policy, and valuation policy, may constitute a legitimate economics act.

The constitutional chain is:

`Authority → Capability → Policy → Standing → Claim → Trust → Verification → Reputation → Attestation → Consensus → Governance → Voting → Federation → Economics → Decision → Action`

Economics governs how sovereign constitutional systems allocate value only as a traceable governed output. It never replaces required standing, evidence, policy coverage, trust evaluation, verification requirements, reputation signals, attestation endorsements, consensus agreement, governance authority, voting legitimacy, or federation relationships, and it never directly authorizes action.

## Economics legitimacy

An economics act is legitimate only when all of the following are traceable:

1. a canonical economics authority and owner;
2. a valid rights assignment satisfying the rights policy;
3. a valid obligations record satisfying the obligations policy;
4. valid asset classification where assets are involved;
5. valid consumption accounting where consumption is recorded;
6. valid settlement where settlement is required;
7. valid treasury governance where treasury funds are involved;
8. valid valuation where value is computed;
9. every lifecycle transition, challenge, and revocation; and
10. the current economics status separately from preserved historical records.

No economics act may be created without rights validation. No economics act may transfer value without settlement policy validation. Settlement transfers value scope only; it does not transfer sovereignty.

## Constitutional doctrines

**Value ≠ Currency** — Constitutional value is a governed signal derived from authority, capability, consumption, and governance. It is not a market currency, token, or coin.

**Rights ≠ Assets** — Constitutional economic rights are governance entitlements. They are not asset ownership records, property claims, or accounting entries.

**Consumption ≠ Ownership** — Consuming a governed resource does not transfer constitutional ownership of that resource.

**Settlement ≠ Payment** — Constitutional settlement is a governed confirmation that economic obligations are satisfied. It is not a payment transaction, wire transfer, or on-chain payment.

## GCU and SCU

**GCU (Governance Compute Unit)** and **SCU (Sovereign Compute Unit)** are constitutional economic primitives used to measure and account for governance and sovereign activity, respectively. They are not currencies, tokens, or financial instruments. GCU and SCU are governed by the GCU Authority (ECO-0009) and SCU Authority (ECO-0010) respectively.

## Constitutional properties

Economics is rights-gated, obligations-governed, asset-classified, consumption-accounted, settlement-validated, treasury-governed, valuation-computed, lifecycle-controlled, challengeable where cataloged, revocable where cataloged, historically preserved, and constitutionally evolvable. An economics act never transfers sovereignty. An economics act never creates market value.

## Scope boundary

This framework governs economics legitimacy and constitutional records only. It does NOT implement cryptocurrency, token issuance, blockchain treasury, market pricing, payment gateway, exchange system, revenue engine, financial accounting, payroll, ERP integration, billing runtime, or tokenization runtime.
