# Federation Constitution

**Constitution Version:** v17.0

## Constitutional purpose

**Federation** is a constitutionally governed relationship between sovereign constitutional systems that establishes recognition, trust, coordination, delegation, and cooperation boundaries. Federation answers **how sovereign constitutional systems interact without surrendering sovereignty**. Federation is not governance, voting, consensus, execution, or economics.

## Sovereign federation rule

A federation relationship may exist, but only a federation authority cataloged in `FEDERATION-AUTHORITIES.md`, operating under its recognition policy, trust policy, delegation policy, capability policy, and governance policy, may constitute a legitimate federation act.

The constitutional chain is:

`Authority → Capability → Policy → Standing → Claim → Trust → Verification → Reputation → Attestation → Consensus → Governance → Voting → Federation → Decision → Action`

Federation governs inter-sovereign relationships only as a traceable governed output. It never replaces required standing, evidence, policy coverage, trust evaluation, verification requirements, reputation signals, attestation endorsements, consensus agreement, governance authority, or voting legitimacy, and it never directly authorizes action.

## Federation legitimacy

A federation act is legitimate only when all of the following are traceable:

1. a canonical federation authority and owner;
2. a valid recognition record satisfying the recognition policy;
3. a trust level assignment satisfying the trust policy;
4. delegation validation where delegation is exercised;
5. capability sharing validation where capabilities are shared;
6. governance policy compliance where federated governance is invoked;
7. every lifecycle transition, challenge, and revocation; and
8. the current federation status separately from preserved historical records.

No federation act may be created without recognition validation. No federation act may delegate authority without delegation policy validation. Delegation transfers authority scope only; it does not transfer sovereignty.

## Constitutional properties

Federation is recognition-gated, trust-governed, delegation-bounded, capability-scoped, governance-constrained, lifecycle-controlled, challengeable where cataloged, revocable where cataloged, historically preserved, and constitutionally evolvable. A federation act never transfers sovereignty. A federation act never copies authority from one sovereign system to another.

## Federation ≠ Merger

Federation and merger are constitutionally distinct. Two sovereign constitutional systems may cooperate through federation without becoming a single constitutional system. Each sovereign system retains its own constitution, authorities, and governance structure throughout a federation relationship.

## Recognition ≠ Trust

Recognition and trust are constitutionally distinct. A sovereign system may recognize another sovereign system as a valid participant without trusting it. Recognition acknowledges existence and validity; trust assigns confidence levels.

## Trust ≠ Delegation

Trust and delegation are constitutionally distinct. A sovereign system may trust another sovereign system without delegating authority to it. Trust governs confidence levels; delegation governs authority scope.

## Delegation ≠ Sovereignty Transfer

Delegation of authority does not transfer sovereignty. A sovereign system that delegates authority to another sovereign system retains its own constitution, governance, and all undelegated authority.

## Scope boundary

This framework governs federation legitimacy and constitutional records only. It does not implement a cross-chain federation runtime, blockchain bridges, runtime federation protocols, economic settlement, treasury federation, network federation protocols, or execution federation.
