# Governance Constitution

**Constitution Version:** v13.0

## Constitutional purpose

**Governance** is a constitutionally governed process through which valid authority, standing, claims, trust, verification, reputation, attestation, and consensus produce legitimate collective direction. Governance answers **how collective legitimacy becomes collective authority**. Governance is not consensus, voting, federation, runtime execution, or economic settlement.

## Sovereign governance rule

A consensus determination may exist, but only a governance authority cataloged in `GOVERNANCE-AUTHORITIES.md`, operating under its proposal policy, motion policy, mandate policy, and outcome policy, may constitute a legitimate governance act.

The constitutional chain is:

`Authority → Capability → Policy → Standing → Claim → Trust → Verification → Reputation → Attestation → Consensus → Governance → Decision → Action`

Governance may produce direction only as a traceable collective authority output. It never replaces required standing, evidence, policy coverage, trust evaluation, verification requirements, reputation signals, attestation endorsements, consensus agreement, decision authority, or explainability, and it never directly authorizes action.

## Governance legitimacy

A governance act is legitimate only when all of the following are traceable:

1. a canonical governance authority and owner;
2. a governance proposal satisfying the proposal policy;
3. a governance motion satisfying the motion policy;
4. consensus satisfaction meeting the governance consensus requirement;
5. a governance mandate recording the authorized scope;
6. a governance outcome recording the result;
7. every lifecycle transition, challenge, expiration, and revocation; and
8. the current governance status separately from preserved historical records.

No governance act may produce a mandate without satisfying its consensus requirement. No governance act may override standing, capability, policy, claim evidence, verification requirements, reputation signals, or attestation endorsements.

## Constitutional properties

Governance is proposal-governed, motion-bound, consensus-requiring, mandate-creating, outcome-recording, purpose-bound, non-transferable, challengeable where cataloged, expirable where cataloged, revocable where cataloged, historically preserved, and constitutionally evolvable. A governance act never copies direction from one subject, context, proposal, or authority to another.

## Consensus ≠ Governance

Consensus and governance are constitutionally distinct:

| Consensus Established | Proposal Valid | Motion Passed | Governance Legitimate | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| No | No | No | No | No agreement; no direction |
| Yes | No | No | No | Agreement exists; no governance proposal |
| Yes | Yes | No | No | Proposal admissible; motion not passed |
| Yes | Yes | Yes | Yes | Governed collective authority established |

All four states are constitutionally valid. Decision authorities may consume consensus and governance acts as independent inputs.

## Governance ≠ Voting

Voting may be one governance mechanism. Governance is broader than voting. The governance framework governs how collective legitimacy is established and how it becomes collective authority. Voting mechanisms are downstream instruments of governance, not governance itself.

## Governance ≠ Execution

Governance authorizes direction. Execution performs runtime action. A mandate records authorized direction. It does not execute action. Mandate existence does not imply runtime execution.

## Governance is an input, not a bypass

Governance may influence decisions. Governance may not override:

- Authority
- Capability
- Policy
- Standing
- Claim Evidence
- Verification Requirements
- Reputation Signals
- Attestation Endorsements
- Consensus Requirements

Governance is a governed input to decision-making. It is never a constitutional bypass.

## Scope boundary

This framework governs governance legitimacy and constitutional records only. It does not implement a voting engine, governance runtime, proposal runtime, mandate runtime, execution runtime, federation engine, economic settlement, treasury governance, or blockchain governance.
