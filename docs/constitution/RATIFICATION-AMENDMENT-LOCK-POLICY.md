# Ratification Amendment Lock Policy

**Constitution Version:** v1.0

## Purpose

Prevent uncontrolled constitutional drift during ratification.

## Lock Rules

- No new constitutional domain may be introduced during ratification.
- Only remediation, parity, audit, ratification, and release changes are allowed during ratification.
- Any post-ratification constitutional change must use the amendment process.
- v1.0 becomes the sovereign baseline.
- Future changes become v1.x or v2.0 according to amendment class.

## Versioning Rule

- Development versions v2.0–v18.0 were construction-era constitutional development versions.
- AOC Constitution v1.0 is the first ratified sovereign baseline.
- Future versions after ratification follow: v1.1, v1.2, v1.3 for minor amendments; v2.0 for major constitutional amendments.
