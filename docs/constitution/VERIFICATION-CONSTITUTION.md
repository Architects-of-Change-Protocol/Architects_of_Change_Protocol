# Verification Constitution

**Constitution Version:** v10.0

## Constitutional purpose

**Verification** is a governed determination that evidence has been evaluated using an authorized method and satisfies the requirements of a verification authority. Verification answers **whether an assertion has been independently validated**. Verification is not authority, capability, policy, standing, claim, trust, or decision.

## Sovereign verification rule

Assertions may exist and trust signals may be active, but only a verification type cataloged in `VERIFICATION-AUTHORITIES.md`, evaluated under its evidence policy using an authorized method, issued by its authorized authority, and maintained through the constitutional lifecycle may constitute a governed verification.

The constitutional chain is:

`Authority → Capability → Policy → Standing → Claim → Trust → Verification → Decision → Action`

Verification may influence a decision only as a traceable validation input. It never replaces required standing, evidence, policy coverage, trust evaluation, decision authority, or explainability, and it never directly authorizes action.

## Verification legitimacy

A verification is legitimate only when all of the following are traceable:

1. a canonical verification authority and owner;
2. an authorized verification method from the method catalog;
3. evidence satisfying source, integrity, freshness, minimum, and threshold rules;
4. a valid lifecycle path through `Pending Verification` before `Verified`;
5. every expiration, revocation, retirement, and decision reference; and
6. the current verification status separately from preserved historical records.

No verification may become `Verified` without evidence evaluation, an authorized method, and a verified threshold. No verification may be self-issued unless its authority explicitly authorizes self-verification.

## Constitutional properties

Verification is evidence-backed, method-backed, purpose-bound, non-transferable, time-aware, expiring where cataloged, revocable where cataloged, historically preserved, and constitutionally evolvable. A verification never copies status from one subject, context, claim, or authority to another.

## Trusted ≠ Verified

Trust and verification are constitutionally independent:

| Trust | Verification | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Low | No | Weak Assertion |
| High | No | Trusted But Unverified |
| Low | Yes | Verified But Untrusted |
| High | Yes | Strong Assertion |

All four states are constitutionally valid. Decision authorities may consume trust and verification as independent inputs.

## Scope boundary

This framework governs verification legitimacy and constitutional records only. It does not implement a verification, identity, trust, reputation, voting, federation, governance, policy, decision, claims, authentication, or authorization runtime.
