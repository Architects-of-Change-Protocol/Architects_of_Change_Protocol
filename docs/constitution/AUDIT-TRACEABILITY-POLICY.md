# Audit Traceability Policy

**Constitution Version:** v1.0

## Preamble

Audit traceability is the governed requirement that every constitutional artifact, authority, scanner, test, lifecycle record, violation, and amendment can be linked to its constitutional source. Traceability is a necessary condition for constitutional integrity and certification readiness.

## Artifact Traceability

Every constitutional artifact must trace to the following dimensions:

| Traceability Dimension | Description | Verification Method |
|---|---|---|
| Authority | Every artifact must be owned by a cataloged constitutional authority. | Authority catalog cross-reference |
| Amendment | Every artifact must be created or modified by a ratified amendment. | Amendment catalog cross-reference |
| Version | Every artifact must declare the current Constitution version. | Version parity scanner |
| Scanner | Every governance obligation must be enforced by a registered scanner. | Scanner registry cross-reference |
| Test | Every scanner must be covered by a registered test. | Test registry cross-reference |
| Domain | Every artifact must belong to a defined constitutional domain. | Domain policy cross-reference |

## Scanner Traceability

Every scanner must be traceable to:

- The domain it governs
- The violation codes it emits
- The artifacts it inspects
- The amendment that established its obligation

## Test Traceability

Every test must be traceable to:

- The scanner it covers
- The domain it validates
- The fixture behavior it exercises

## Lifecycle Traceability

Every lifecycle record must be traceable to:

- The authority that authorized the transition
- The evidence supporting the transition
- The amendment establishing the lifecycle semantics

## Violation Traceability

Every violation code must be traceable to:

- The domain it belongs to
- The scanner that emits it
- The remediation policy that governs its resolution
- The amendment that established it
