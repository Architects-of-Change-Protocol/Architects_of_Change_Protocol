# Trust Issuance Policy

**Constitution Version:** v13.0

## Issuance rule

Trust issuance requires valid applicable standing, a valid claim, successful evidence evaluation, the cataloged trust authority, and an immutable trust record. Failure of any prerequisite denies issuance. Issuance creates a purpose-bound confidence signal; it does not transfer authority or authorize action.

No trust may be self-issued unless the applicable catalog rule says `Self-Issuable: Yes` and identifies the explicit constitutional authorization. Silence means self-issuance is forbidden.

## Trust record

Every issued record contains a unique record ID, trust ID, subject and context, standing reference, claim reference, evidence evaluation reference, issuer, calculation version, current confidence, lifecycle state, creation time, review time, and complete lineage. Confidence is not transferable between subjects or contexts; reassignment requires a new issuance evaluation and record.
